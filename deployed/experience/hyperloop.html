Michigan Hyperloop

Michigan Hyperloop is designing the way for a new form of transportation to connect people and ideas for quickly, efficiently and easily.

What is Hyperloop

Hyperloop is a vision of the next generation of ground transportation popularized by Elon Musk. By creating a network of near-vacuum tubes connecting large cities and developing high speed pods capable of travelling upwards of 800mph, it would allows for more efficient transportation to radically change the way we view distance and lead to a more physically connected future. The research and development of Hyperloop Technology has been outsourced Universities and private entities throughout the world, and this technology comes to a head every summer at the SpaceX hosted Hyperloop Pod Competition. I had the pleasure of attending Competition III in Hawethorne, CA in July of 2018

What I do

On the Michigan Hyperloop Team, I was the embedded systems lead and a member of the race crew. I was reponsible for leading the design of the a robust and functional electronics system for the 2019 SpaceX Hyperloop Pod Competition. I spearheaded the research and integration of a 20 sensor array capable of safetly accelerating and decelerating to a max speed of 300mph. We used a system with rotary encoders and accelerometers as well as photoreflective sensors to track our telemetry within the tube, pressure transducers to monitor our high pressure system and actuate brakes, and laser distance sensors to track our pitch, roll and yaw along the I-Beam.
